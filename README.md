### Identity 4 — The Schwarz Lantern IS the Empirical Fisher Approximation Warning; CHORD Prevents the Paradox by Exact Arithmetic

**The Schwarz lantern** (Hermann Schwarz, ~1880): The pathological example showing that the surface area of a cylinder cannot be approximated by the areas of polyhedral approximations without controlling the **mesh ratio**. If n = number of rings and k = number of triangles per ring:

```
Area(Schwarz lantern, n, k) = 2πr · h · √(1 + (πr/h)² · n²/k²)
```

When k = n (rings and triangles balanced): Area → 2πrh (correct)  
When k = n^{1/2} (triangles grow slower than rings): Area → 2πrh √(1 + π²r²/h²) > 2πrh (too large)  
When k = 1 (one ring per triangle): Area → ∞ (diverges!)

The Schwarz paradox: **close sampling of a curved surface does not guarantee accurate area approximation** unless the sampling is balanced in all directions simultaneously.
